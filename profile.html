Overview

Who We Are

Centre For Special Abilities (CeSA) is a Community Based Organization founded in January 2015 in Nansana, Uganda. We work with children with disabilities, supporting them toward independent living through therapy, special needs education, and vocational training.

Children with disabilities in Uganda are among the most overlooked groups — many lack access to basic health services and education, and remain invisible in national policy. CeSA exists to change that, one child at a time.

Organisation Facts

Key Information

What We Do

Areas of Focus

CeSA provides a wide range of services designed to support the whole child — from assessment and therapy to education and spiritual development.

🔍

Child Psycho-Educational Assessment

Identifying each child's needs and strengths through structured evaluation.

🤲

Occupational Therapy

Helping children develop the everyday skills needed for independent living.

💬

Speech & Language Therapy

Supporting communication development for children with speech difficulties.

🏃

Physiotherapy

Integrated physio sessions for mobility and physical development.

📚

Special Needs Education

Classes from Nursery through Senior Four, tailored to each child's learning needs.

🌱

Basic Skills & Vocational Training

Practical skills in agriculture, music, food preparation and home management.

📣

Advocacy & Networking

Amplifying the voices of children with disabilities and their families.

🤝

Community Mobilisation

Sensitisation and empowerment within the wider Nansana community.

✝️

Spiritual & Religious Development

Nurturing the whole person, including values, faith and character.

Our Goals

Project Objectives

  1. To conduct community mobilisation for sensitisation, and child psycho-educational assessment for intervention.
  2. To promote children's health and advocate for their rights.
  3. To develop basic vocational skills in affordable and manageable fields like agriculture, music, dance & drama, food preparation and home management.
  4. To act as a model centre for hands-on training of students from higher institutions of learning.
  5. To promote environmental conservation strategies and practices for sustainable development.
